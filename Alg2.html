WORLD OF 10th GRADE HUMANITIES

Course Overview

Welcome to Algebra II. It's not exactly a 10th grade class, but Freshmen, Sophomores and Juniors alike all take the class so yeah I'm counting it. In this class the pythagorean theorem from Geometry will be used and material learned will build on Algebra I while preparing for PreCalculus and beyond. In class the routine is just to complete the Do Now, take class notes from the document camera and then complete homework. Tests happen generally every week on the material said week and there is a final test at the end of each semester. While material may be difficult at times, if you remember to take notes and complete assignments, you should end the school year with an A in that class.

Syllabus

Topic Description

Quadratics/basics

The first unit is really just an introduction to Algebra 2 and the basics. You'll be introduced to what standard form, vertex form and factored form are. Once you learn that you will learn how to factor, how to FOIL and then finally the vertex and quadratic formulas. Also you will look at parabolas and look at some higher degree equations including cubics and quartics, but there's generally no factoring by grouping yet.

Rationals and Radicals

The second unit then reviews on how to simplify radicals before you use them in equations where you generally have to just isolate the square root and then solve. Also you will learn how to convert from radicals to rational exponents and while touching on the subject of rational exponents, will also touch on the number sets out there as well as irrationals and imaginary numbers.

Exponents/Logarithms

The third unit will go over how to simplify expressions with exponents in them, before moving on to exponential equations. So far you will only worked with annual, semi-annual, quarterly, daily and compouding interests in equations as well as log and natural log (ln). Also graphing exponentials is involved and you will also be introduced to horizontal and vertical asymtotes as well as domain and range of graphs. Additionally there will be a few lessons on Explicit and Recursive formulas.

Unit Circle

The final unit will require some knowledge from geometry. After reviewing the trig ratios and the pythagorean theorem, you will have to apply it to the Unit Circle. First you will learn about how to convert from degrees to radians and back as well as coterminal angles. Also while working with solving for radius, theta and coordinate values in a circle, you will also be introduced to rationalizing denominators, something that will come up later on in PreCalculus and beyond. Finally the year will conclude in learning about Sin and Cos waves as well as a review for the second semester final.
