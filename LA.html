WORLD OF 10th GRADE HUMANITIES

Course Overview

Welcome to 10th grade Language Arts, where much of the material corresponds to what you learn in European History. Each Unit unit features a writing assigment of some sort along with reflection, as well as occasional art projects from time to time. Additionally, there is a End of Semester Portfolio at the end of each semester, where you compile all the essays/reflections from said semester into one portfolio along with a semester evaluation/reflection. So expect a lot of writing in this class! Other than that, a couple of books are assigned at times and sometimes there's even the luxury of movies. Generally, if you pay attention and turn all your work in on time, this class should be a breeze.

Syllabus

Topic Description

Ancient Greece

The first unit of the class, the stories you can expect to read are "The Iliad", featuring the perspectives of Achilles and Hecter respectively, as well as several of Aesops Fables. There's two essays for the Iliad and the main themes to look out for are characteristics of communities, consequences for dissenters who don't conform and how opposing forces are highlighted in a community by the author.

Medieval Era

The second unit of the class features stories including: "Lais of the Werewolf", featuring a man turned Bisclarvet/uncivilized, and several portions from "Le Morte d'Arthur" which feature the destruction of everything due to the lack of chivalry. The essay for both stories is pretty hefty so look out for common themes in both, such as common Chivalric values highlighted or behavior disapporoved traditionally.

Reformation

The third unit of the class features the stories "Don Quixote" which is about an old man who tries to revive chivalrous values of the past and "Macbeth" which is about a soldier who tried to become king due to what he heard from 3 witches. Both films feature in class movies and for the "Macbeth" essay, watch out for any themes and lessons highlighted by Shakespeare throughout the book.

Romantic Era

The fourth unit of the class features several Poems by William Wordsworth, Taylor Coleridge, Lord Byron, etc as well as a movie on Jane Austen's novel, "Emma". For the poems, just remember to fill out the packets when needed and for the movie "Emma" make sure to pay attention to character action and take notes if needed as there will be a seminar afterwards on values brought up in the movie.

Victorian Era

The fifth unit of the class features several Sherlock Holmes stories by Arthur Conan Doyle as well as the play/movie "The Importance of being Earnest" by Oscar Wilde. You will be reading the Sherlock Holmes stories in groups though the project can be individual or group and involves creating a video, game, poster, etc on one of the stories. As for "The Importance of being Earnest" pay attention to how Oscar Wilde mocks or highlights certain aspects of Victorian Era society for the essay afterwards.

Modern Era

The sixth and final unit of the class features "Lord of the Flies" a book about how a group of boys stranded on an island turn savage over time. Make sure to fill out all worksheets given to you and read each chapter carefully so that you can can choose a theme that occurs throughout the book and write about it in the essay, such as the symbol of the conch. Additionally there will be an episode of the "Twilight Zone" to correspond to the gang mentality/savageness shown in the book and a seminar afterwards on questions on the book so make sure to READ THE BOOK!

General Tips/Advice

  • When available, take the honors assignments. Its worth it!
  • Your essay's thesis should be at the end of the introductory paragraph and supported throughout the essay.
  • On the topic of essays, don't just list too much evidence. Elaboration of said evidence is key!
  • Finally the conclusion should connect to Modern Society, not just restate everything in the introduction.
  • For the DRP and SBAC in the year, take your time and make sure to get enough sleep for those tests!
  • Talk to Mr. Maher, he's an awesome teacher and a cool guy if you get to know him. But remember, teachers are people too so don't bother him with late work, especially near the end of the school year!
