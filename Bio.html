WORLD OF 10th GRADE HUMANITIES

Course Overview

Welcome to Biology, the class most 10th graders take regardless of what academy they are in. In Biology class much of what you learn is delivered via powerpoint and reading pathfinders and you should take notes in your journals in preparation for the quizzes on each unit, as well as the Biology EOC at the end of the school year. Additionally, there are journal checks often so you kind of have to do work in your journal and fill in the table of contents according to what Mr. Swarts assigns. Overall, if you complete all the assignments and pay attention in class, especially to the types of questions on the EOC, Biology should be a pretty easy course and the EOC, an easy one.

Syllabus

Topic Description

Organic Molecules

The first unit of the class. You will be introduced to what the Organic molecules are and what makes an Organic Molecule (High energy C-C or C-H bonds). You will also learn the monomers of each orgainic molecule (Amino Acids for Proteins, Glucose for Carbohydrates and Glycogen/Fatty Acids for Lipids) and generally this first unit is pretty simple as it introduces you to what'll be needed for the next few units. Additionally you learn how to write conclusions here as well.

Cellular Functions

This unit expands on the first unit. You will learn about Digestion and how Polymers are broken down into Monomers as well as Biosynthesis and how monomers are recontructed for the needs of the body and in the case of plants, reconstructed into other organic biomolecules. Cellular respiration and the breaking down of glucose for energy in the form of ATP will also be taught here. Additionally you will learn about the phospholipid bilayer and how molecules diffuse into and out of a cell via osmosis to achieve equilibrium. Modeling of Biosynthesis and digestion will better help understand the transition from monomer to polymer.

DNA the 4th Biomolecule

This unit will introduce DNA, as well as DNA replication of cells known as Mitosis. There will be drawing activities to teach the double helix, the nitrogenous bases Adenine, Thymine, Cytosine, and Guanine as well as RNA and its unique base Uracil. Afterwards you will learn about proteins and enzymes and how DNA codes for proteins with the help of RNA.

Genetics and Inheritance

This unit will introduce Meiosis and how gametes came to be. You will utilize the Punnett Square in modeling what offsprings will recieve what genetics from their parents and also learn about mutation in the process. Afterwards, you will learn about the history of the Earth via the Geologic Clock and learn how evolution/natural selection over time caused certain orgainisms to be how they currently are and how they've changed from their ancestors. The anole lizard activity will be the main focus at that point in modeling evolution.

Ecosystems

This final unit will introduce where Carbon Pools are in an Ecoystem, (producers, consumers, decomposers, atmosphere) and what the relationship between them is. A flash game called Sunny Meadows will be the focus at the start of the unit and after you learn about how to trace carbon and energy in ecosystems and how big carbon pools are, there will be a presentation in groups. Also, finals will be an oral presentation in front of the class of something you learned in the school year.
